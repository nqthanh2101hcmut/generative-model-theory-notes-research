# Entropy-Regularized Distributional Optimization

## Basic problem

Let \(\rho_0\) be a reference distribution. Consider

\[
\min_{\rho\in\mathcal{P}(\mathcal{X})}
\mathbb{E}_{\rho}[\ell(x)]
+
\lambda\mathrm{KL}(\rho\|\rho_0).
\]

This problem appears in diffusion alignment, reinforcement learning from preferences, and distributionally regularized learning.

## Formal solution

Assume \(\rho\ll\rho_0\). Let

\[
\frac{d\rho}{d\rho_0}(x)=r(x).
\]

Then

\[
\mathrm{KL}(\rho\|\rho_0)
=
\int r(x)\log r(x)\,d\rho_0(x).
\]

The objective becomes

\[
\int \ell(x)r(x)d\rho_0(x)
+
\lambda\int r(x)\log r(x)d\rho_0(x),
\]

subject to

\[
\int r(x)d\rho_0(x)=1.
\]

The solution has Gibbs form

\[
\frac{d\rho^\star}{d\rho_0}(x)
=
\frac{\exp(-\ell(x)/\lambda)}{Z},
\]

where

\[
Z=\int \exp(-\ell(x)/\lambda)d\rho_0(x).
\]

## With constraints

If constraints are added,

\[
\mathbb{E}_{\rho}[c_j(x)]\le b_j,
\]

the optimal density becomes

\[
\frac{d\rho^\star}{d\rho_0}(x)
\propto
\exp\left(-\frac{1}{\lambda}
\left[\ell(x)+\sum_j\alpha_j^\star c_j(x)\right]\right).
\]

## Research relevance

This form explains how reward, constraints, and KL regularization reshape the distribution generated by a base model.
